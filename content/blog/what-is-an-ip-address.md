An **IP address** (Internet Protocol address) is a numeric label assigned to each device on a network. It allows packets to be routed from source to destination across the internet.

## IPv4 and IPv6

- **IPv4** uses 32 bits, often written as four decimal octets (for example `203.0.113.10`).
- **IPv6** uses 128 bits and solves address exhaustion with a much larger space.

## Public vs private

Your home router usually has a **public** IP on the WAN side and assigns **private** addresses (such as `192.168.x.x`) to phones and laptops. When you visit a website, the server typically sees your public IP, not your private LAN address.
